In the absence of evidence to support use of vocational rehabilitation and low certainty evidence for use of work site evaluations and modifications, we conditionally recommend use of these approaches. The Voting Panel recognized that the following factors should be considered in implementing work site evaluations and modifications: 1) the employee/employer relationship regarding health-specific variables and confidentiality, 2) the patient's comfort with disclosure of RA to the employer, 3) the requirements of the Americans with Disabilities Act and the Family and Medical Leave Act, 4) the heterogeneity of employer resources and employee job responsibilities, and 5) the variable availability of experienced work and ergonomics specialists.
