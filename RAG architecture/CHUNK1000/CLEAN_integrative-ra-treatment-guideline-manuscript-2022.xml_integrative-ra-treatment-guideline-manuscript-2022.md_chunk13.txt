The timing of the use of interventions, guidance on intervention selection, and education on how to use these interventions should be considered. Involving an occupational or physical therapist can aid these processes and ensure patient safety. The Voting Panel recognized cost and burden as barriers to the use of these interventions. For patients who are currently employed or want to become employed, we conditionally recommend use of vocational rehabilitation (training programs to support employment) over no vocational rehabilitation. For patients who are currently employed or want to become employed, we conditionally recommend work site evaluations and/or modifications over no work site evaluations and/or modifications.
